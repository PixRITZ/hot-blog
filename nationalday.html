National Cheese Fondue Day

Today is all about cheese fondue!

April 11, 2026 Sam Tun

Today is April 11th, and it's National Cheese Fondue Day! Let's dip right into the topic for today!

If you don't know what fondue is, it's basically melted cheese kept in a pot that is constantly warm, to keep it gooey and melted. Sometimes people will even use chocolate for chocolate fondue, but today is specifically cheese fondue. It's often paired with breads, vegetables, and meats to be dipped with. Based on my experience, I've had my family do a cheese fondue before, and I can say it's a very cozy and relaxing little experience that is worth a try. Maybe you and your family members can have one. My personal favorite thing to dip with cheese was warm and toasted bread! Usually chicken or ham are meats enjoyed with fondue, which I've had ham with it.

Cheese fondue started to become popular in the 1800s in France. The name actualy comes from "fondre" which means to melt in French. When fresh food was not available, they would have cheeses that were ready to be melted and served with bread as the cold months went by. In the 1930s, the Swiss Cheese Union declared fondue to be the national dish of Sweden. The types of fondue change from country to country, so you can do your own research and see what's different as the cheeses used in the fondue are different.
