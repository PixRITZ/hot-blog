Leukemia: Out of the Question!

Cancer didn't stand a chance against me

April 8, 2026 Sam Tun

You can never tell when something small can create a huge impact on your life. For me, it was my diagnosis of Acute Myeloid Leukemia. My life was normal one day, and the next I would be spending months in the hospital.

It was October 14, 2025 and I was sitting in my English class knowing that my mom was coming to pick me up for a doctor's appointment. Once I was called to the office, I told my friends, "See you tomorrow!" Little did I know that this would be the last time I saw those classmates for a long time. I got in the car, and my mom surprised me with a four pack of Crumbl Cookies. I was excited and immediately started tasting them. As we pulled up to the doctor's office, I tucked away the cookies and got out of the car. We had noticed small strange dots on my skin and wanted to get a closer look, so that's what the doctor did. She ordered that we go the hospital not too far across the street to get my labs checked. I remember coming back out feeling like they had taken gallons of blood; I was so weak. The past few weeks also felt more challenging to keep up with, especially because I participate in Taekwondoe. To make up for this, my mom bought me a Freddy's milkshake, and I enjoyed it on the way home.

When we got back home, I did my typical routine where I would study a bit and play some video games. My mom called for me to come downstairs and eat dinner, which I specifically remember being chicken and plain white toast. I took the plate upstairs and ate it happily. It was until the doctor called around 9:30pm, which was very late at night. She ordered us to head to the hospital as soon as possible. I took a quick shower and wrapped the chicken and toast neatly and shoved it in the back of the fridge, hoping to eat it as a snack later. But I couldn't help but keep thinking about what I had heard on the phone. Cancer was the suspect of all this. Dread. It was pure dread. That long, half an hour car ride had my heart racing. I didn't know what was going to happen. I didn't know what was going on. I knew nothing.

Once we arrived at the hospital, they took us up to the floor where I was shown my room. I was going to have to stay overnight, which I had never done before, so I was a bit nervous. I was getting hungry, so the nurse got some chicken tenders and french fries for me. I had the Minecrat Movie playing on the hospital TV while I ate, so it wasn't all that boring. They eventually poked me to place an IV, which went fine and only hurt a little bit. From there, they hooked me up to saline so I was getting the fluids I needed to stay hydrated. A few hours later around 2am, they had me get into a wheelchair and took me downstairs for an x-ray.

After the x-ray, I could finally try and get some sleep. The bed was actually more comfortable than it looks. I may have not gotten the best sleep that night, but it was something. Until the next morning when I met all my doctors and other people on the team. It was a tough chat, but I learned that I had Acute Myeloid Leukemia. I was in disbelief because cancer was something I would have never ever thought of getting to me. But for treatment, it would be receiving chemotherapy day and night for a few weeks. I had a line installed that same week and would constantly get woken up during the night as the care assistants grabbed my vitals. All of this led to four cycles of treatment, three of which are complete as of the time I'm making this post. Not long after the third admission, I had a bone marrow biopsy. This biopsy took place on the 21st of January, 2026. After a few days of stressful waiting, I was left with news that put a large smile on my face. There were no signs of Leukemia in my body. A four month journey led to this. I can officially say I had beaten cancer!

Even though at the moment I'm writing this and I'm cancer-free, I still had one more cycle to complete. This was the bone marrow transplant phase. Now that you have learned about my tough battle against Leukemia, I will make a post in the future discussing the recovery process of the bone marrow transplant and my ongoing fight against GVHD.
